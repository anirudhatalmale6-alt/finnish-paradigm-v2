1. Purpose

These Consultant and Trainer Terms apply to external trainers, consultants, facilitators, assessors, content creators, reviewers, curriculum writers, video presenters and workshop leaders engaged by FCEI.

A separate signed contract may override or supplement these Terms.

2. Professional standards

Trainers and consultants must deliver services professionally, ethically, accurately and in line with FCEI values, safeguarding obligations, course objectives, client agreements and applicable law.

3. No false claims

Trainers must not claim that FCEI, its courses or certificates are officially Finnish-government accredited, university-awarded or state-approved unless FCEI has provided written authorisation and evidence.

4. Confidentiality

Trainers must keep confidential:

  • learner data;
  • school documents;
  • institutional reports;
  • consultancy diagnostics;
  • business pricing;
  • unpublished course materials;
  • internal FCEI processes;
  • client communications;
  • safeguarding information.

5. Data protection

Trainers must process personal data only for authorised FCEI purposes. Trainers must not download, copy, store, reuse, upload to unauthorised tools, or share personal data outside approved systems.

6. Safeguarding

Trainers must follow FCEI safeguarding procedures. Any concern involving a child, vulnerable learner, abuse, risk of harm or unsafe content must be escalated immediately to the FCEI safeguarding contact.

7. Intellectual property

Unless a signed agreement states otherwise, materials created specifically for FCEI may be owned by or licensed to FCEI. Trainers must not reuse FCEI materials for competing services without written permission.

Trainers must ensure any third-party materials they provide are properly licensed and credited.

8. AI and avatar use

Trainers must not use AI tools to process personal data, learner data, confidential documents, child images, voices, school records or client materials unless FCEI has approved the tool and lawful basis.

Trainers must not create synthetic media using a person’s image, voice or identity without permission.

9. Conduct

Trainers must not harass, discriminate, exploit learners, make inappropriate contact, request personal payments outside FCEI systems, or damage FCEI’s reputation.

10. Quality assurance

FCEI may review trainer materials, session recordings, learner feedback, assessment decisions, workshop delivery and consultancy outputs for quality and compliance.

11. Payments

Trainer fees, payment dates, cancellation terms, expenses, taxes and deliverables must be set out in a written contract, statement of work or purchase order.

12. Termination

FCEI may suspend or terminate trainer access for breach of these Terms, safeguarding risk, confidentiality breach, data misuse, poor performance, false claims, misconduct or legal risk.
