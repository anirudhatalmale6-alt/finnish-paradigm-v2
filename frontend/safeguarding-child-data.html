1. Purpose

FCEI is primarily designed for adult professional development users. However, some services may involve schools, parents, teachers, institutional cohorts or educational materials connected to children or learners under 18.

This Policy explains the minimum safeguarding and children’s data controls required where children may access the platform or where users upload learner-related material.

2. Default position

FCEI should not knowingly collect children’s personal data unless the relevant product, school contract or learning activity is designed and approved for that purpose.

3. Age and access controls

Where under-18 users may access the platform, developer must implement:

  • age gate or institutional enrolment control;
  • parental/guardian or school consent where required;
  • child-appropriate privacy notice;
  • restricted public profile visibility;
  • no behavioural advertising to children;
  • high privacy defaults;
  • no open public messaging for minors;
  • content reporting tools;
  • safeguarding contact information.

4. Uploading learner material

Users must not upload:

  • identifiable child images;
  • learner medical records;
  • safeguarding records;
  • behaviour incident reports;
  • full names of children;
  • home addresses;
  • confidential school records;
  • sensitive family information;
  • assessment data that identifies children;
  • videos of learners;
  • voice recordings of learners;

unless they have permission, a lawful basis and a clear course/institutional reason.

5. Anonymisation

Where learner evidence is needed for professional development, users should anonymise material by removing:

  • full names;
  • faces;
  • school IDs;
  • addresses;
  • parent names;
  • exact dates of birth;
  • identifiable classroom displays;
  • metadata in files.

6. Safeguarding reports

FCEI is not an emergency safeguarding authority. If a child is at immediate risk, users must contact local emergency services or the relevant school/local safeguarding authority.

If safeguarding-risk content is uploaded to FCEI, staff should escalate it to the designated safeguarding contact and restrict access while it is reviewed.

7. Staff access

Only authorised staff with a genuine need should access files that may contain children’s data. Access must be logged.

8. Retention

Children’s data should be minimised and deleted as soon as it is no longer needed, unless legal, institutional or safeguarding requirements require retention.
