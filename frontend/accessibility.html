1. FCEI accessibility commitment

FCEI aims to make its website, LMS, course pages, video modules, downloads and professional development resources accessible to as many users as reasonably possible.

The development target is WCAG 2.2 AA, subject to legal review, technical feasibility and platform limitations.

2. Accessibility features to implement

The developer must support:

  • keyboard navigation;
  • visible focus indicators;
  • readable headings and semantic HTML;
  • alt text for meaningful images;
  • captions or transcripts for video content where possible;
  • sufficient colour contrast;
  • accessible form labels and error messages;
  • responsive layouts;
  • screen-reader compatible navigation;
  • pause/stop controls for moving or auto-playing content;
  • no critical text hidden behind avatars, overlays or captions;
  • accessible PDF production where possible.

3. Video accessibility

FCEI video modules should include, where practical:

  • captions;
  • transcript text;
  • clear audio;
  • readable on-screen text;
  • no obstruction of avatar face by captions or overlays;
  • consistent slide structure;
  • pause points and clear instructions.

4. Known limitations

FCEI may have limitations in:

  • older PDF downloads;
  • third-party embedded media;
  • AI-avatar rendering tools;
  • user-uploaded files;
  • third-party payment pages;
  • legacy templates.

Known limitations should be documented in the admin Accessibility Log.
