1. Purpose

This Data Processing Addendum, or DPA, applies where an institution, school, company, training centre, NGO, ministry or organisation uses FCEI to enrol learners, manage cohorts, receive reports or deliver training involving personal data.

2. Parties

Customer / Institution: [Insert Institution Name]

Supplier / Processor: [Insert FCEI Legal Entity Name], trading as FCEI

3. Roles

The institution is usually the controller of personal data for its staff, learners, participants and institutional users.

FCEI is usually the processor where it processes personal data on behalf of the institution to deliver platform services, course access, reporting, certificates, support and administration.

In some cases, FCEI may act as an independent controller for its own legal, billing, security, analytics, fraud-prevention or compliance purposes.

4. Processing subject matter

FCEI processes personal data to provide online courses, LMS access, professional development, assessment, evidence upload, certificates, support, reporting, communications and platform administration.

5. Categories of data subjects

  • institutional administrators;
  • teachers;
  • school leaders;
  • trainers;
  • consultants;
  • learners/participants;
  • support contacts;
  • billing contacts;
  • workshop attendees.

6. Categories of personal data

  • name;
  • email address;
  • organisation;
  • job title;
  • country;
  • course progress;
  • assessment results;
  • certificate records;
  • uploaded evidence;
  • support messages;
  • login and security logs;
  • payment or invoice contact information where applicable.

7. Sensitive and children’s data

The institution must not upload sensitive data, safeguarding records, medical records, child images, learner records or special category data unless it has a lawful basis, permission and written agreement with FCEI.

8. Processor obligations

FCEI will:

  • process personal data only for agreed purposes;
  • use suitable confidentiality commitments;
  • apply reasonable technical and organisational security measures;
  • assist with data rights where reasonably possible;
  • assist with security incidents where required;
  • delete or return data at contract end subject to legal retention;
  • maintain a subprocessor list;
  • require appropriate terms from subprocessors;
  • keep audit records relevant to processing.

9. Institution obligations

The institution will:

  • provide lawful instructions;
  • ensure it has a lawful basis for enrolment and data sharing;
  • give privacy information to its users where required;
  • avoid uploading unnecessary personal data;
  • obtain parental/guardian consent where required for minors;
  • manage user access promptly;
  • notify FCEI of unauthorised users or account changes.

10. Security measures

Minimum measures include:

  • HTTPS;
  • access controls;
  • role-based permissions;
  • admin MFA;
  • secure password hashing;
  • audit logs;
  • restricted file access;
  • backups;
  • incident logging;
  • least-privilege administration.

11. Subprocessors

FCEI may use subprocessors for hosting, storage, payments, email, video, analytics, AI-avatar media, support and security. The current list should be published at `/subprocessors`.

12. International transfers

Where data is transferred internationally, FCEI will use appropriate safeguards where required.

13. Data breach notification

FCEI will notify the institution without undue delay after becoming aware of a confirmed personal-data breach affecting institutional data, subject to verification and legal requirements.

14. Deletion and return

At the end of the contract, the institution may request deletion or export of institutional data, subject to legal, tax, security, certificate-verification and backup retention requirements.

15. Order of precedence

If this DPA conflicts with a signed institutional contract, the signed contract controls unless the contract states otherwise.
