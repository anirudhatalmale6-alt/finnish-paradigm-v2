Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

FCEI ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data responsibly. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, how we use it, how we store it, and your rights regarding your data.

This policy applies to all users of FCEI platform, including teachers, school leaders, institutional administrators, and visitors to our website.

By using our Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. For detailed information about our data protection practices, please also see our Data Protection Policy.

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Account information: Name, email address, password (stored as a cryptographic hash), job title, and organisational affiliation.
  • Profile information: School or institution name, role (teacher, school leader, administrator), subject specialisms, and professional development interests.
  • Course activity: Assessment scores, quiz responses, progress data, module completion records, and certificate issuance details.
  • Intervention tracking data: Where you use our intervention tracking tools, you may input pseudonymised student codes, intervention types, and outcome data. See Section 6 for details on student data handling.
  • Communication data: Messages sent through contact forms, support requests, and feedback you provide.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage data: Pages visited, features used, time spent on modules, and interaction patterns within the platform.
  • Device and browser information: Browser type, operating system, screen resolution, and device type.
  • Log data: IP address, access times, and referring URLs.

2.3 Payment Information

Payment card details are collected and processed exclusively by Stripe, our third-party payment processor. We do not store, access, or process your full card number, expiry date, or CVV. We receive only a transaction confirmation, the last four digits of your card, and billing address information necessary for our records.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the personal data we collect for the following purposes:

  • Service delivery: To create and manage your account, provide access to purchased courses, track your progress, and issue certificates.
  • Assessment and reporting: To score assessments, generate progress reports, and provide personalised learning recommendations.
  • Communication: To respond to your enquiries, send essential service notifications (e.g., course updates, password resets), and, with your consent, send marketing communications about new courses and resources.
  • Platform improvement: To analyse usage patterns and improve our courses, platform features, and user experience.
  • Legal compliance: To fulfil our legal obligations, enforce our Terms of Use, and protect against fraud.
  • School reporting: For institutional licence holders, to provide aggregated and anonymised usage reports to the licence administrator as agreed in the institutional licence terms.

4. Data Storage and Security

Your personal data is stored in an encrypted database with the following security measures:

  • All data is encrypted at rest and in transit using industry-standard encryption protocols (TLS 1.2+ for transit, AES-256 for storage).
  • Passwords are stored using secure one-way cryptographic hashing (bcrypt) and are never stored in plain text.
  • Access to personal data is restricted to authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis.
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments are conducted.
  • Authentication tokens are stored in the browser's localStorage and are scoped to your session.

While we implement robust security measures, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but are committed to protecting your data to the highest practicable standard.

5. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may process your data:

  • Stripe: Payment processing. Stripe processes your payment information in accordance with their own privacy policy and PCI DSS standards. See Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Analytics providers: If configured, we may use analytics tools to understand platform usage. See our Cookie Policy for details.
  • Email service providers: For sending transactional and, where consented, marketing emails.

We do not sell, rent, trade, or otherwise share your personal data with third parties for their own marketing purposes. Your data is never sold.

6. Student Data and Intervention Tracking

FCEI is a teacher-facing platform. We do not directly collect data from students or children.

Where teachers or school leaders use our intervention tracking tools, they may input data about student interventions. This data is handled as follows:

  • Students are identified only by pseudonymised codes (e.g., "Student A", "S001"), never by name or other directly identifying information.
  • Teachers and schools are responsible for ensuring that any data entered complies with their own institution's data protection policies and applicable laws.
  • Intervention data is stored securely and is accessible only to the teacher who entered it and, where applicable, their institutional administrator.

For full details on our approach to student data, see our Safeguarding Policy and Data Protection Policy.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to provide the Service and fulfil the purposes described in this policy:

  • Account data: Retained for the duration of your active account plus 2 years after account closure or last activity, to allow for account reactivation and certificate verification.
  • Course progress and certificates: Certificate records are retained indefinitely for verification purposes. Course progress data is retained for 3 years after course completion.
  • Payment records: Retained for 7 years as required by financial record-keeping regulations.
  • Intervention tracking data: Retained for the duration of the active account. Deleted within 90 days of account closure.
  • Analytics data: Aggregated and anonymised data may be retained indefinitely. Identifiable analytics data is retained for no more than 26 months.

You may request earlier deletion of your data at any time, subject to our legal obligations (see Section 8).

8. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access: You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to erasure: You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to our legal obligations and legitimate interests.
  • Right to restrict processing: You may request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability: You may request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: You may object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 11. We will respond to your request within 30 days.

9. GDPR Compliance

FCEI is designed with GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) principles in mind. We process personal data based on the following lawful bases:

  • Contract: Processing necessary to deliver the courses and services you have purchased.
  • Consent: For marketing communications and non-essential cookies/analytics.
  • Legitimate interest: For platform security, fraud prevention, and service improvement.
  • Legal obligation: For financial record-keeping and regulatory compliance.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom and believe we have not adequately addressed your data protection concerns, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

For more details, see our Data Protection Policy.

10. Cookies and Local Storage

Our platform uses cookies and browser local storage to provide essential functionality and, where configured, to analyse usage. For full details on what cookies and local storage we use and how to manage them, please see our Cookie Policy.

11. Contact for Data Requests

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, wish to exercise your data rights, or have concerns about how your data is handled, please contact us:

  • Email: [privacy@thefinnishparadigm.com - to be confirmed]
  • Contact form: Contact page
  • Data Protection Officer: [DPO contact details - to be confirmed before launch]

We aim to respond to all data-related enquiries within 30 days.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make significant changes, we will notify you by email (if you have an account) and update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Your continued use of the Service after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

This policy should be reviewed by a qualified legal professional before commercial launch.
