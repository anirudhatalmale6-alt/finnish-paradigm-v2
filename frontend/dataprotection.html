Data Protection Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Introduction and Scope

The Finnish Paradigm™ is committed to protecting the personal data of all individuals who interact with our platform, in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK Data Protection Act 2018, and other applicable data protection legislation.

This Data Protection Policy sets out the principles, procedures, and responsibilities that govern how we collect, process, store, and protect personal data. It applies to:

  • All personal data processed by The Finnish Paradigm™ in connection with the delivery of our services.
  • Data relating to teachers, school leaders, institutional administrators, and other platform users.
  • Pseudonymised student data entered into our intervention tracking tools by authorised users.
  • All staff, contractors, and third-party processors acting on our behalf.

This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy (which is user-facing and explains data practices to end users) and our Safeguarding Policy.

2. Data Protection Principles

In line with GDPR Article 5, The Finnish Paradigm™ adheres to the following data protection principles. All personal data shall be:

  • Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently: We will always have a valid lawful basis for processing personal data and will be clear with users about how their data is used.
  • Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes: Data is collected only for the purposes described in our Privacy Policy and is not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes.
  • Adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary (data minimisation): We collect only the minimum data required to deliver our services effectively.
  • Accurate and kept up to date: We take reasonable steps to ensure personal data is accurate and provide users with the ability to correct their information.
  • Kept for no longer than necessary (storage limitation): Data retention periods are defined and enforced as set out in our Privacy Policy.
  • Processed with appropriate security (integrity and confidentiality): We implement technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or damage.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

The Finnish Paradigm™ processes personal data under the following lawful bases:

3.1 Contract (GDPR Article 6(1)(b))

Processing necessary for the performance of our contract with you, including:

  • Creating and managing your account.
  • Providing access to purchased courses, toolkits, and assessment instruments.
  • Tracking course progress and issuing CPD certificates.
  • Processing payments through Stripe.
  • Providing customer support related to your purchases.

3.2 Consent (GDPR Article 6(1)(a))

Processing based on your freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent, including:

  • Sending marketing communications about new courses, resources, and platform features.
  • Setting non-essential analytics cookies (see our Cookie Policy).
  • Participating in optional surveys, feedback requests, or research studies.

You may withdraw consent at any time by updating your communication preferences in your account settings, clicking the unsubscribe link in any marketing email, or contacting us directly. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

3.3 Legitimate Interest (GDPR Article 6(1)(f))

Processing necessary for our legitimate interests, provided these are not overridden by your rights, including:

  • Platform security, fraud prevention, and abuse detection.
  • Analysing aggregated usage data to improve our courses and platform features.
  • Internal record-keeping and business administration.
  • Ensuring network and information security.

We conduct a legitimate interest assessment (LIA) for each processing activity relying on this basis to ensure our interests do not override the rights of data subjects.

3.4 Legal Obligation (GDPR Article 6(1)(c))

Processing necessary to comply with a legal obligation, including:

  • Financial record-keeping and tax compliance.
  • Responding to lawful requests from regulatory authorities.
  • Safeguarding reporting obligations where applicable.

4. Data Minimisation

We apply the principle of data minimisation across all aspects of the platform:

  • Account registration: We collect only the fields necessary for account creation and service delivery (name, email, organisation, role). We do not require unnecessary demographic information.
  • Course activity: We track progress at the module level (completion status, assessment scores) and do not record granular behavioural data such as keystrokes or mouse movements.
  • Intervention tracking: Our system is designed to accept only pseudonymised student codes. The platform does not include fields for student names, dates of birth, addresses, or other directly identifying student information. If a user attempts to enter identifiable student data, they do so in breach of our terms and safeguarding policy.
  • Payment data: We do not process or store full payment card details. All payment processing is handled by Stripe.

5. Student Code Pseudonymisation in Intervention Tracking

The intervention tracking feature of The Finnish Paradigm™ is designed with privacy by design principles:

  • Pseudonymisation approach: Students are identified only by codes (e.g., "S001", "Year4-A", "Student Alpha"). These codes have no inherent meaning outside the teacher's own records.
  • No re-identification capability: The Finnish Paradigm™ does not hold or have access to the mapping between student codes and real student identities. This mapping is maintained solely by the school or teacher using their own secure systems.
  • Data controller vs. data processor: For intervention tracking data, the school or teacher is the data controller (they determine the purposes and means of processing student-related data). The Finnish Paradigm™ acts as a data processor, processing this data only on behalf of and under the instructions of the data controller.
  • Purpose limitation: Intervention data is processed solely for the purpose of supporting the teacher's professional practice in tracking and evaluating student interventions. It is not used for marketing, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to the service.

6. Data Processing Agreements for Schools

Where schools or educational institutions use The Finnish Paradigm™ under an institutional licence, and where teachers within that institution enter pseudonymised student data into our intervention tracking tools, a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is required.

Our standard DPA covers:

  • The nature, purpose, and duration of processing.
  • The types of personal data processed and categories of data subjects.
  • The obligations and rights of the data controller (school) and data processor (The Finnish Paradigm™).
  • Technical and organisational security measures in place.
  • Sub-processor arrangements and notification procedures.
  • Procedures for handling data subject access requests received by the processor.
  • Data return and deletion upon termination of the agreement.
  • Audit rights for the data controller.

Schools and institutional licence holders can request a copy of our standard DPA by contacting us at the details provided in Section 11. We are open to negotiating specific terms where required by the institution's data governance framework.

7. Data Security Measures

The Finnish Paradigm™ implements the following technical and organisational measures to protect personal data:

Technical Measures

  • Encryption of data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).
  • Secure password hashing using bcrypt with appropriate salt rounds.
  • Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorised personnel.
  • Regular security patching and vulnerability scanning.
  • Database backups encrypted and stored securely with controlled access.
  • Input validation and parameterised queries to prevent injection attacks.
  • Rate limiting and brute-force protection on authentication endpoints.

Organisational Measures

  • Data protection training for all staff and contractors with access to personal data.
  • Confidentiality agreements for all personnel.
  • Access to production data limited to essential personnel on a need-to-know basis.
  • Regular review of access permissions.
  • Documented incident response procedures.

8. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, The Finnish Paradigm™ will follow these procedures:

8.1 Internal Response

  • Upon discovery of a suspected breach, the incident will be immediately escalated to the Data Protection Officer (or designated responsible person).
  • An investigation will be initiated to determine the nature, scope, and severity of the breach.
  • Containment measures will be implemented as quickly as possible to prevent further data loss.

8.2 Supervisory Authority Notification

  • Where a breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, as required by GDPR Article 33.
  • The notification will include the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate number of data subjects affected, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

8.3 Data Subject Notification

  • Where a breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will notify affected data subjects without undue delay, as required by GDPR Article 34.
  • Notification will include a clear description of the breach, the data involved, the potential consequences, and the steps affected individuals can take to protect themselves.

8.4 School Notification

  • Where a breach involves intervention tracking data entered under an institutional licence, we will notify the school's designated data protection contact within 24 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in addition to the supervisory authority notification.
  • This enables the school, as the data controller for student-related data, to fulfil its own notification obligations.

8.5 Record-Keeping

All data breaches, regardless of whether they meet the threshold for notification, will be documented in our breach register, including the facts relating to the breach, its effects, and the remedial action taken.

9. International Data Transfers

The Finnish Paradigm™ primarily processes and stores data within [hosting region - to be confirmed]. Where personal data is transferred to a country outside the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Adequacy decisions: Transfers to countries that the European Commission or UK government has determined provide an adequate level of data protection.
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): Where no adequacy decision exists, we use European Commission-approved Standard Contractual Clauses to ensure appropriate protection.
  • Additional safeguards: Where necessary, supplementary measures such as encryption and pseudonymisation are applied to protect data during transfer.

Our primary third-party processor, Stripe, processes payment data in accordance with their own international data transfer mechanisms. See Stripe's Privacy Policy for details.

10. Data Subject Rights

Under GDPR and applicable data protection legislation, individuals have the following rights. The Finnish Paradigm™ has procedures in place to fulfil these rights within the required timeframes:

  • Right of access (Article 15): You may request confirmation of whether we process your personal data and, if so, a copy of that data. We will respond within 30 days.
  • Right to rectification (Article 16): You may request correction of inaccurate data. Many data fields can be corrected directly through your account settings.
  • Right to erasure (Article 17): You may request deletion of your data where there is no compelling reason for continued processing. This right is subject to exceptions for legal obligations and the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Article 18): You may request that we restrict the processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Article 20): You may request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (e.g., JSON or CSV).
  • Right to object (Article 21): You may object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

Requests should be directed to our Data Protection Officer (see Section 11). We will verify your identity before processing any request and will respond within 30 days, or inform you if an extension is necessary.

For data subject access requests relating to intervention tracking data entered under an institutional licence, we may need to coordinate with the school (as the data controller) before fulfilling the request.

This Data Protection Policy is reviewed at least annually, or more frequently in response to:

  • Changes in data protection legislation or regulatory guidance.

  • Data breach incidents or near-misses.
