Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how FCEI uses cookies and similar technologies (including browser local storage) when you visit and use our platform. It describes what these technologies are, why we use them, and your rights to control their use.

This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy, which provides further details on how we handle your personal data.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide reporting information.

In addition to traditional cookies, modern web applications (including FCEI) also use browser local storage (also known as HTML5 localStorage). Local storage works similarly to cookies but can store larger amounts of data and remains until explicitly cleared. We are transparent about our use of both technologies in this policy.

3. Types of Cookies and Storage We Use

3.1 Essential (Strictly Necessary)

These are required for the platform to function and cannot be disabled without breaking core features.

  • Authentication token (localStorage): Stores your login session token so you remain signed in as you navigate the platform. This is stored in your browser's localStorage rather than a traditional cookie. It is removed when you sign out.
  • Session management: Maintains your active session state, including which course module you are viewing and assessment progress within a session.
  • Security tokens: CSRF (Cross-Site Request Forgery) protection tokens that help prevent malicious actions on your account.
  • Cookie consent preference: Stores your cookie consent choice so we do not ask you repeatedly.

3.2 Functional

These enhance your experience by remembering choices you make while using the platform.

  • Course preferences: Remembers your preferred display settings, such as video playback speed, subtitle preferences, or module view layout.
  • Dashboard settings: Stores your preferred dashboard configuration and filter selections.
  • Language/locale preferences: If applicable, stores your language or regional format preferences.

Functional cookies and storage are not strictly necessary but significantly improve your user experience. Disabling them may result in reduced functionality.

3.3 Analytics (if configured)

If analytics services are enabled, they may set cookies to help us understand how visitors use the platform.

  • Page views and navigation patterns: Which pages are visited, how long users spend on each page, and navigation paths through the platform.
  • Feature usage: Which features and tools are most frequently used, helping us prioritise improvements.
  • Device and browser information: Aggregated data about the devices and browsers used to access the platform.

Analytics cookies are only set with your consent. They do not collect personally identifiable information and are used solely for platform improvement.

3.4 Third-Party Cookies

Some third-party services integrated with the platform may set their own cookies:

  • Stripe (payment processing): Stripe may set cookies during the checkout process for fraud prevention and payment security. See Stripe's Cookie Policy.
  • Video hosting providers: If course videos are hosted on a third-party platform, that provider may set cookies when you view video content.

We do not control third-party cookies. Please refer to the relevant third party's cookie policy for details.

4. Consent Mechanism

When you first visit FCEI, you will see a cookie consent notice. This notice allows you to:

  • Accept all cookies: Enables all cookie categories, including analytics and functional cookies.
  • Accept essential only: Enables only strictly necessary cookies and local storage required for the platform to function. Analytics and non-essential functional cookies are not set.

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clearing your browser cookies and local storage, or by using the cookie settings link in the website footer.

Essential cookies and local storage (including your authentication token) cannot be disabled while using the platform, as they are required for it to function. If you do not wish to accept essential cookies, you should not use the platform.

5. Local Storage Disclosure

FCEI uses browser local storage (HTML5 localStorage) for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: Your JSON Web Token (JWT) or session token is stored in localStorage to maintain your logged-in state. This is the primary authentication mechanism for the platform.
  • User preferences: Display settings, course progress checkpoints, and dashboard configuration.
  • Offline data: Minimal course navigation data that allows pages to load faster on repeat visits.

Local storage data persists until you explicitly clear it (via browser settings or by signing out, which clears authentication data). Unlike cookies, local storage data is not automatically sent to the server with every request, which provides a minor privacy benefit.

6. How to Manage and Delete Cookies

You have control over cookies and local storage on your device. Here is how to manage them in common browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data. To clear localStorage: Developer Tools (F12) > Application tab > Local Storage.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data. To clear localStorage: Developer Tools (F12) > Storage tab > Local Storage.
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data. localStorage can be cleared from the same interface.
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data. To clear localStorage: Developer Tools (F12) > Application tab > Local Storage.

Please note that clearing cookies and local storage will sign you out of FCEI and may reset your preferences. You will need to sign in again and reconfigure any personal settings.

For more detailed instructions, visit aboutcookies.org.
