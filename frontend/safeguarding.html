Safeguarding Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Our Commitment

FCEI is committed to safeguarding the welfare of children and young people. While our platform is designed for use by adult professionals (teachers, school leaders, and education administrators), we recognise that the work undertaken on our platform directly relates to improving outcomes for children and that our intervention tracking tools involve the handling of student-related data.

This Safeguarding Policy sets out our approach to child protection, professional boundaries, and the responsible handling of data that relates to children and young people.

2. Platform Scope and Audience

FCEI is a teacher-facing professional development platform. It is important to understand the following:

  • Our platform is designed exclusively for adult professionals. Students, children, and young people are not intended users of the platform and should not be given access to it.
  • No course content is designed for direct delivery to or interaction with children through the platform.
  • All account holders must be 18 years of age or older.
  • The platform does not include any direct communication features between platform users and children.

Where our courses include strategies, frameworks, or resources intended for classroom implementation, these are designed to be adapted and delivered by qualified teachers within their own professional context and institutional safeguarding frameworks.

3. Intervention Case Data Handling

Our intervention tracking tools allow teachers and school leaders to record, monitor, and analyse student interventions as part of their professional practice. We take the handling of this data extremely seriously:

3.1 Data Minimisation

  • The platform is designed to use pseudonymised student codes only (e.g., "Student A", "S001", "Year 4 - Code 12"). Teachers must not enter students' real names, dates of birth, addresses, or other directly identifying information into the platform.
  • The mapping between student codes and real identities should be maintained separately by the school, using their own secure systems, and is never stored on FCEI platform.
  • Intervention records should contain only the minimum data necessary: student code, intervention type, dates, and outcome measures.

3.2 Access Controls

  • Intervention data entered by a teacher is accessible only to that teacher and, where applicable, their designated institutional administrator.
  • We do not aggregate, analyse, or share individual student intervention data for any purpose other than providing the Service to the user who entered it.
  • Anonymised and aggregated data (with no possibility of re-identification) may be used for platform improvement and research, but only at a statistical level.

4. DBS and Vetting

FCEI provides online professional development content. Our team members do not have unsupervised contact with children.

  • Our responsibility: Any FCEI staff or contractors who may, in the course of their duties, have access to identifiable student data (even pseudonymised) will be subject to appropriate background checks as required by applicable law.
  • Employing institution's responsibility: DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) checks, vetting, and barring decisions for teachers and school staff who use the platform are the responsibility of their employing school or institution. FCEI does not conduct employment checks on behalf of schools.
  • Schools using our platform under an institutional licence should ensure that all staff granted access have appropriate safeguarding clearances in accordance with their local regulatory requirements.

5. Professional Boundaries

Users of FCEI are expected to maintain professional boundaries at all times:

  • Course content, assessment data, and intervention tracking tools must be used solely for legitimate professional development and school improvement purposes.
  • Users must not use the platform to share, discuss, or distribute any content that could be harmful to children.
  • Case studies, intervention examples, and discussion of student scenarios within courses must use pseudonymised or fictional examples only.
  • Users must not attempt to identify students referenced in anonymised case studies or aggregated data shared within the platform.

Any user found to be using the platform in a manner inconsistent with professional conduct or child safeguarding will have their access immediately suspended and, where appropriate, reported to the relevant authorities.

6. Appropriate Use of Assessment Data

FCEI provides assessment instruments designed for teacher professional development and, in some cases, for use in classroom practice. When assessment data relates to students (even in pseudonymised form), users must:

  • Use assessment data only for its intended purpose: identifying learning needs, planning interventions, and measuring progress.
  • Not use assessment data to label, rank, or stigmatise individual students.
  • Store and share assessment results only through secure, approved channels in accordance with their school's data protection policy.
  • Ensure that assessment results are interpreted within their proper professional context and with appropriate expertise.
  • Not export or share raw student assessment data from the platform except in accordance with their institution's data governance policies.

7. School Implementation Safeguards

Schools implementing strategies, frameworks, or interventions learned through FCEI courses should:

  • Ensure that all classroom implementations are aligned with their existing safeguarding policy and procedures.
  • Conduct their own risk assessments before implementing new intervention approaches.
  • Ensure that the designated safeguarding lead (DSL) is aware of any new assessment or intervention approaches being introduced.
  • Maintain their own records of the mapping between student codes used on the platform and actual student identities, in compliance with their data protection obligations.
  • Review and approve the use of FCEI within their school's broader technology and data governance frameworks.

FCEI provides professional development content and tools, but individual schools are responsible for ensuring safe and appropriate implementation within their specific context.

8. Reporting Concerns

If you encounter any content on the platform that you believe poses a safeguarding risk, or if you have concerns about the conduct of any user, please report it immediately:

  • Email: [safeguarding@thefinnishparadigm.com - to be confirmed]
  • Contact form: Contact page (select "Safeguarding Concern")

All safeguarding concerns will be treated with the utmost seriousness and confidentiality. We will:

  • Acknowledge your report within 24 hours.
  • Investigate the concern promptly.
  • Take appropriate action, which may include suspending accounts, removing content, and reporting to the relevant authorities where required by law.

If you are a teacher or school leader and have a concern about a child's welfare that has arisen in the course of using our platform or implementing strategies learned through our courses, you should follow your school's own safeguarding procedures and report to your designated safeguarding lead (DSL) in the first instance.

9. Review and Updates

This Safeguarding Policy is reviewed annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in legislation, best practice, or our Service.

This policy should be reviewed by a qualified legal professional before commercial launch.
