1. What cookies are

Cookies and similar technologies store or access information on your device. They may help a website work, remember preferences, secure accounts, measure traffic, personalise content, embed videos or support marketing.

2. Cookie categories used by FCEI

FCEI must organise cookies into these categories:

CategoryPurposeConsent needed?
Strictly necessaryLogin, checkout, security, cookie preference storage, load balancingUsually no
FunctionalRemember preferences, language, display settingsUsually yes unless essential
AnalyticsUnderstand traffic, course usage, error patterns, improvement needsYes where required
MarketingAds, remarketing, conversion tracking, campaign attributionYes
Video / third-party mediaEmbedded video players, AI-avatar media, captions, external media toolsYes where non-essential

3. Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are needed for the platform to operate. They may include:

  • session authentication;
  • security and fraud prevention;
  • cart or checkout state;
  • cookie consent storage;
  • load balancing;
  • password reset or login protection.

These should be documented in the Cookie CMS.

4. Non-essential cookies

Analytics, marketing, remarketing, behavioural advertising, heatmaps, social pixels and non-essential third-party media must not load until the user has given consent where required.

5. Cookie consent banner requirements

The banner must include:


Accept All
Reject All
Manage Choices
Save Preferences

The user must be able to change preferences later using the footer link Cookie Settings.

6. Cookie preference centre

The preference centre must show:

  • cookie category;
  • provider;
  • cookie name;
  • purpose;
  • duration;
  • whether it is first-party or third-party;
  • whether it is essential;
  • link to provider policy where applicable.

7. Consent records

FCEI should store:


consent_id
user_id or anonymous_id
cookie_policy_version
categories_accepted
categories_rejected
timestamp
ip_address
user_agent
country

8. Withdrawing consent

Users can withdraw or change cookie consent at any time through the footer Cookie Settings link.

9. Cookies used by third parties

Third-party services may include hosting providers, Stripe, analytics tools, video players, AI-avatar tools, email tools or support widgets. FCEI must list each enabled third-party tool in the Cookie CMS before launch.
